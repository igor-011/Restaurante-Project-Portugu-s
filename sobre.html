Le'President

The Le Président restaurant, opened by the renowned chef Erick Jacquin in 2019, is a reference in French cuisine in São Paulo, Brazil. Located in an upscale neighborhood of the city, the establishment provides a refined and sophisticated gastronomic experience. In addition to the high quality of the food, attentive and professional service is a trademark of Le Président. The team of trained staff is always ready to offer personalized recommendations, assist in choosing wines that harmonize with the dishes, and ensure that customers have a memorable experience. Le Président is a notable restaurant in the gastronomic scene of São Paulo, bringing the authenticity and refinement of French cuisine through the talent of Chef Erick Jacquin and his team. It is an ideal destination for those who appreciate haute cuisine and wish to experience special moments in an exquisite and welcoming environment.

After bankruptcy, celebrity chef Érick Jacquin shines again in the kitchen

Known to the general public for his work on the MasterChef program, the chef closed his last restaurant due to being buried in debts. Now, he returns to the stoves with Président, inaugurated in December in São Paulo.

Despite his talent as a chef, when the first Brazilian edition of the program was launched in 2014, Jacquin was a bankrupt person, drowned in debts, facing several labor lawsuits, and away from the stoves.

It was his success with MasterChef (and other fame-related products such as lectures, commercials, and other television programs) that allowed Jacquin to pay off his debts (estimated to exceed R$ 1 million) and have a restaurant of his own again. Jacquin swears he learned his lesson. The first is to never deal with the financial or human resources aspect of the business again.

"That's with my partner," he says, after narrating how the bank manager made him take unnecessary loans when he was in charge of his last restaurant, La Brasserie, closed in 2013.

Since the closure of La Brasserie, due to – believe it or not – lack of customers and also because of debts, Leone, then a loyal customer of the chef, had been sounding out the chef to return to the kitchen. "He always told me he wasn't ready. Until last year, when he hinted at the possibility," says Leone.

Jacquin's yes made the businessman invest heavily. "It was a lot of money, twice what I expected," he says, without revealing the figures. In the kitchen, there was no skimping: state-of-the-art equipment is at the chef's and his team's disposal. The kitchen is open to the dining room, and Jacquin works – and shines – on an illuminated platform similar to the MasterChef set.

The decor of the dining room bets on red, the color of many French brasseries. On one of the walls, various personal items of Jacquin are displayed, such as a menu from Le Coq Hardy, the first restaurant where he worked in Brazil, or a photo of the chef when he was young, taken for the cover of a magazine.

On another wall, there is a gallery with paintings of all French presidents. "Emmanuel Macron is in the center, but he will lose this position in the next election," points out Jacquin. At the back of the room, a huge mirror brings a sense of spaciousness to the place.

But it is the kitchen that will dictate the success of the venture. Jacquin's partners in previous projects always questioned the amount spent on some ingredients, and Jacquin's kitchen is filled with foie gras, scallops, caviar, and truffles. They are part of the belief that a good chef is rarely a good administrator, and few know how to calculate the cost of a dish.

Président, by Chef Jacquin, is elected the best restaurant of the year in Robb Report Brazil in 2021


The Président, led by Chef Jacquin, is the big winner of the first edition of the Robb Report Brazil Food and Drink Awards. The awards, held at a special lunch at the winning restaurant on Tuesday (9), featured 11 categories, also recognizing chefs, buffets, and experiences.

With a jury composed of Alexandra Loras, Catherine Petit, Daniel Mendez, Edu Guedes, Luiza Brunet, Marcos Quintela, and the Publisher of Robb Report Brazil, Celia Pompeia, the winners were:

  • Seen (Hotel Tivoli) – Best Bar
  • L'Épicerie – Best Buffet
  • Helena Rizzo – Best Chef
  • Stefan Behar Sucré – Best Pastry Shop
  • Chez Claude – Best Authorial Cuisine
  • Dinner in the Sky – Best Experience
  • Skye – Unique Hotel Best Hotel Restaurant
  • Président – Best Restaurant
  • Gabriel Coelho – Best Emerging Chef
  • Evvai – Veuve Clicquot Innovation
  • Morena Leite – Veuve Clicquot Sustainability

"Robb Report Brazil has in its DNA to publish the best of the best in lifestyle, design, automotive, travel, and gastronomy. From special experiences to the spirit of a new era," evaluates Celia Pompeia. "The award, which honors the best of the best in gastronomy, completes the appreciation of this very special segment. Congratulations to everyone who not only won but also were finalists. I am very proud. In December, we will have a special edition with the winners," she concludes.

The voting, which recognized the winners, took place in October. For each category, there were three carefully selected options for choice by the Editorial Board of Robb Report Brazil, an international title published by Doria Editora in the country. The Robb Report Brazil Food and Drink Awards had the sponsorships of BMW, Líder Aviação, and Veuve Clicquot.
